# Session 07.03 — Executive Session, Adjournment & Post-Meeting Review

**Phase:** 1 — MVP | **Block:** 07 — Live Meeting Manager
**Dependencies:** 07.02
**Estimated tasks:** 11

---

## Description

Build the executive session flow (enter/exit with legal citation, pause public recording), dual adjournment methods (formal motion and chair declaration), post-meeting review summary, tabled items carry-forward queue, meeting data lock on completion, and the JSON data export for the AI minutes generation pipeline.

## Tasks

1. Create ExecutiveSessionDialog: enter executive session with legal citation (Maine 1 M.R.S.A. 405(6) categories), record start time, pause public recording
2. Implement executive session behavior: mark items discussed as "executive session" in meeting record, exclude from public minutes/portal, record only that executive session occurred and the legal citation
3. Create executive session exit: return to public session, record end time
4. Implement dual adjournment support (Q7): "Motion to adjourn" (formal — goes through motion/vote flow) AND "Chair declares meeting adjourned" (informal — operator clicks button, confirms)
5. Record adjournment time and method in meeting record
6. Implement meeting end flow: adjournment -> meeting status changes to "completed" -> post-meeting review screen
7. Create PostMeetingReview page: summary of meeting (duration, attendance, items covered, motions/votes recorded, items tabled)
8. Create "items for next meeting" queue: tabled items and items not reached auto-populate for the next meeting's agenda
9. Implement meeting lock: after completion, meeting data becomes read-only (only minutes can be generated from it)
10. Create meeting data export: JSON export of all meeting data (attendance, items, motions, votes, timestamps) for minutes generation pipeline
11. Verify: enter/exit executive session, both adjournment methods work, post-meeting review shows accurate summary, tabled items carry forward, meeting locks on completion

EXECUTIVE SESSION LEGAL CONTEXT (Maine 1 M.R.S.A. 405(6)):
Maine law permits executive session only for specific statutory reasons. The categories under 405(6) are:
- (A) Discussion of the employment, appointment, assignment, duties, promotion, demotion, compensation, evaluation, disciplining, resignation, or dismissal of an official, appointee, or employee
- (B) Discussion of suspension or expulsion of a public school student
- (C) Discussion of a matter which would be within the scope of the "executive session" provisions of FOAA if it related to records
- (D) Discussion of labor contract negotiations, including proposals and counterproposals
- (E) Consultations with a municipal attorney concerning pending or contemplated litigation
- (F) Discussion of information contained in records made, maintained, or received by a body or agency when access by the general public to those records is prohibited
- (G) Discussion or approval of the content of examinations
- (H) Discussion of information provided to the body in confidence

The system must require a legal citation when entering executive session. The citation is recorded in the meeting record.

TASK 1: Create ExecutiveSessionDialog
- File: packages/web/src/components/meeting/ExecutiveSessionDialog.tsx
- Opened from an "Enter Executive Session" button in the AgendaItemDetailPanel (visible when the current item is in an executive_session section or accessible from a menu on any item)
- Dialog fields:
  - Legal Citation: Select dropdown with the 8 categories from 1 M.R.S.A. 405(6):
    - "(A) Personnel matters" — full text shown in a tooltip/description
    - "(B) Student suspension or expulsion"
    - "(C) Records exemption under FOAA"
    - "(D) Labor contract negotiations"
    - "(E) Consultation with legal counsel"
    - "(F) Confidential records"
    - "(G) Examination content"
    - "(H) Confidential information"
  - The legal citation is REQUIRED — the dialog cannot be submitted without selecting a category
  - A "Motion Required" note reminds the operator: "A motion to enter executive session is required before proceeding."
- Before entering executive session, the standard motion/vote flow must occur:
  - The dialog pre-fills a motion: "to move into Executive Session pursuant to Title 1, Section 405(6)([letter])"
  - The motion goes through MotionCaptureDialog -> VotePanel flow (from session 07.02)
  - If the motion passes, the system enters executive session mode
  - If the motion fails, the system remains in public session
- On entering executive session:
  - Record entered_at timestamp
  - Record the statutory_basis (e.g., "1 MRSA 405(6)(E)")
  - Create an EXECUTIVE_SESSION record: id, meeting_id, agenda_item_id, statutory_basis, entered_at, exited_at, entry_motion_id, town_id
  - Display a prominent banner: "EXECUTIVE SESSION IN PROGRESS — Public recording paused"

TASK 2: Implement executive session behavior
- While in executive session:
  - The LiveMeetingPage displays a red/amber banner at the top: "EXECUTIVE SESSION — 1 MRSA 405(6)([letter])"
  - The detail panel shows a simplified view: just the executive session topic title and timer
  - No operator notes, no detailed content capture — the system records ONLY:
    - That executive session occurred
    - The legal citation
    - Entry and exit timestamps
    - Any post-session actions (motions/votes taken after returning to public session)
  - The AgendaNavigationPanel shows the executive session item with a special locked icon
  - If audio recording is eventually added (future block), it would pause during executive session
- Items discussed in executive session are marked with an executive_session flag
- These items are excluded from public minutes and the public portal
- The minutes for executive session will read: "The Board entered executive session at [time] pursuant to [citation]. The Board returned to public session at [time]."

TASK 3: Create executive session exit
- "Return to Public Session" button appears in the banner during executive session
- Clicking it opens a confirmation Dialog: "Confirm return to public session?"
- On confirm:
  - Record exited_at timestamp on the EXECUTIVE_SESSION record
  - Remove the executive session banner
  - The detail panel returns to normal mode
  - Show a prompt: "Were any actions taken after returning from executive session?" with Yes/No buttons
- If "Yes": the operator can record post-session actions using the standard motion/vote flow
  - Post-session motions are linked to the EXECUTIVE_SESSION record via post_session_action_motion_ids array
  - These motions appear in the structured meeting record under the executive session's post_session_actions field
  - Example from Newcastle: after returning from executive session about legal consultation, the board voted to accept an agreement
- If "No": the system advances to the next agenda item
- The executive session duration is displayed in the meeting timer area

TASK 4: Implement dual adjournment support
- Two distinct adjournment buttons in the LiveMeetingPage footer or in a "Meeting Controls" dropdown:

  A) "Motion to Adjourn" (formal):
    - Opens MotionCaptureDialog pre-filled with: motion_text: "to adjourn the meeting", motion_type: "adjourn"
    - Goes through the standard motion/vote flow
    - If the motion passes: meeting ends (Task 6)
    - If the motion fails: meeting continues (this is unusual but legally possible — the board votes not to adjourn yet)
    - Records adjournment method as "motion" with the linked motion_id

  B) "Adjourn Without Objection" (informal / chair declaration):
    - Opens a confirmation Dialog: "The Chair declares the meeting adjourned. Confirm?"
    - Shows the presiding officer's name: "[Presiding Officer Name] adjourns the meeting without objection"
    - On confirm: meeting ends immediately (no motion/vote required)
    - Records adjournment method as "without_objection" with the presiding officer's name

- Both methods are always available — the operator chooses based on the board's convention
- Per Q7: this reflects real-world practice where boards use both conventions depending on formality level

TASK 5: Record adjournment data
- When adjournment occurs (via either method), record:
  - adjournment_method: "motion" | "without_objection"
  - adjournment_time: timestamp (now)
  - adjourned_by: person_id (presiding officer for without_objection, or motion mover for formal)
  - adjournment_motion_id: FK to MOTION record (null for without_objection)
- Store adjournment data on the MEETING record as a JSONB field (adjournment):
  {
    "method": "without_objection",
    "adjourned_by": "person-uuid",
    "adjourned_by_name": "Thomas Kostenbader",
    "motion_id": null,
    "timestamp": "2026-03-08T20:41:00Z"
  }
- This data feeds directly into the structured meeting record for AI minutes

TASK 6: Implement meeting end flow
- After adjournment (either method):
  1. Set meeting status to "completed"
  2. Record ended_at timestamp on the MEETING record
  3. Close any open agenda item transitions (set ended_at on the last AGENDA_ITEM_TRANSITION)
  4. Mark any remaining agenda items that were not reached as status: "deferred"
  5. Navigate to the PostMeetingReview page: /meetings/:meetingId/review
- The transition is: in_progress -> completed
- This transition is irreversible — a completed meeting cannot be returned to in_progress
- All motion and vote data is finalized at this point

TASK 8: Create "items for next meeting" queue
- File: packages/web/src/components/meeting/FutureItemsQueue.tsx
- When a meeting is completed, automatically populate the future items queue for the board:
  - Tabled items: items with status "tabled" are added to the queue with source: "tabled"
  - Deferred items: items with status "deferred" (not reached) are added with source: "deferred"
  - Future agenda items: items from the "Future Agenda Items" section are added with source: "future_queue" (per Q3)
- The FUTURE_ITEM_QUEUE table stores:
  - id, board_id, town_id, source_meeting_id, source_agenda_item_id, title, description, source (tabled/deferred/future_queue), status (pending/placed/dismissed), created_at
- Queue items appear as suggestions when building the next meeting's agenda (in the AgendaBuilder from 06.02):
  - A "Suggested Items" panel shows queued items
  - The operator can "Add to Agenda" (creates an agenda item and marks queue item as "placed") or "Dismiss" (marks as "dismissed" with optional reason)
- Queue items persist until explicitly placed on an agenda or dismissed
- Display in PostMeetingReview: list of items added to the queue with their source

TASK 9: Implement meeting lock
- After a meeting is completed (status: "completed"), all meeting data becomes read-only:
  - MEETING record: no updates except minutes_status and document references
  - AGENDA_ITEM records: no updates
  - MOTION records: no updates
  - VOTE_RECORD records: no updates
  - MEETING_ATTENDANCE records: no updates
  - EXECUTIVE_SESSION records: no updates
- The lock is enforced at the application level:
  - The LiveMeetingPage checks meeting status on load — if "completed", all action buttons are hidden
  - Edit forms for agenda items, motions, etc. are disabled
  - The AgendaBuilder (06.02) shows a read-only view for completed meetings
- Minutes generation (future block) is the only write operation permitted on a completed meeting:
  - MINUTES_DOCUMENT records can be created and edited
  - The meeting record's minutes_status field can be updated
- The lock ensures the integrity of the official meeting record — no retroactive changes to votes, motions, or attendance

TASK 10: Create meeting data export

    - Recusals with reasons
    - Operator notes

  - Adjournment data (method, timestamp, who adjourned)
  - Town profile settings (minutes_style, formality, member_reference_style)
- The JSON structure matches the schema from advisory 2.2 Section 2 exactly — this is the input for the AI minutes generation pipeline
- File is downloaded as: {board-name}-{meeting-date}-meeting-data.json
- The JSON is also stored in Supabase Storage for the API to access during minutes generation
